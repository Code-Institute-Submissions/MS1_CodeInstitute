About

In life it's good to be able to laugh at yourself, in my case, it's necessary. -Quigs

David O’Quigley, or ‘Quigs’ as he is more commonly known, is a self-proclaimed maker: a fixer-of things, an innovator of ideas. By all accounts Quigs is also an excellent problem solver.

Having observed most of the life lessons he’s been given, Quigs has gathered a unique set of skills through various work opportunities and experiences as well as many collaborations throughout the years.

With an eclectic work background that ranges from managing a juggling shop to cycling upwards of 80km a day as a cycle messenger in London, to his most recent venture: programming Arduinos for art installations, Quigs’ skills have been steadily growing - not to mention his ability to talk about himself in the third person.

The journey to better himself is ongoing and Quigs is now studying to become a Full Stack Developer, a job that he knows he will excel in. He is excited to take what he already knows and build on it in order to provide a unique perspective to his problem solving.

Projects

With an eclectic work background it means I have been able to work across several different fields, each requiring their own set of unique skills and problems to be solved. Here are just some of the projects that have moulded me over the years.

Social Cycle

In 2015 myself and some friends entered a hackathon in London. Over the course of a weekend we developed and built a proof of concept cycling product.

The experience was great, working with people from a broad spectrum of backgrounds we were able to compliment each other’s skills perfectly.

The end result was a first place winning wearable smart bike light that would automatically indicate for the user while providing extra visibility for other road users.

Read more about it here.

Pagoda

In the last few years I’ve developed my passion for micro-electronics and smart Internet Of Things devices. These are basically just small, task-specific computers.

I collaborated with an artist to produce Pagoda, a 14m tall hanging sculpture with over 45m of RGB LED lighting all of which was controlled by an Arduino Mega. The piece was toured around a number of music festivals and ultimately seen by thousands.

My role was to help realise the artists goal. I specified the various components that would be needed, taking into consideration the power draw and computing needs and the animations that were required. There was a bit of a learning curve as I hadn’t done anything this big before not to mention the problems we dealt with while installing it on site. This was a hugely rewarding project and has since opened many doors for me.

LCEF - 10 Year Aniversay

While in London I was lucky enough to work as a cycle messenger, arguably one of the best jobs I have ever had. Being a lover of bikes anyway is probably key to helping that.

LCEF, or London Courier Emergency Fund, was set up over a decade ago in order to help support cycle couriers who were injured at work. Due to technically being self-employed when a courier is injured at work that tends to stop their income.

For this project I used my skills as a videographer to produce this short piece highlighting the importance of the LCEF and letting others know what it is they do. This was a fun project to try my hand at filming while on a bike.

Have a look at it here.
